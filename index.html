Intel Data Center Energy Analytics

Shipped
Tableau Data Visualization
May 2026
  • Recommended Central (CENT) as the optimal U.S. data center site for Intel — the only region that is simultaneously a net energy producer and top 5 for renewable generation
  • Built 6 linked Tableau views analyzing hourly generation and demand data by region, covering energy source mix, supply/demand balance over 24 hours, net production, and renewable share
  • Created calculated fields (Net Production, Renewable Energy %, Percent Difference) and an interactive period parameter for dynamic time-window filtering across all views
Recommendation
  • Central (CENT) — the recommended site. It balances energy surplus, grid stability, and renewable usage better than any alternative: net producer with strong wind capacity
  • Mid-Atlantic rejected despite its July surplus: heavy fossil fuel reliance undermines long-term sustainability
  • California rejected despite top-tier solar: runs a net energy deficit, making grid reliability a risk
Key Findings
  • MIDA, SW, CENT, and SE are net energy producers — generation consistently exceeds demand
  • Top 3 regions by renewable %: NW (hydropower), CENT (wind), CAL (solar)
  • CENT is the only region that is simultaneously a net producer and in the top 5 for renewable generation
  • Mid-Atlantic’s largest surplus occurs in July — seasonal, not structural
  • California’s wind peaks ~12 PM–9 PM (cresting ~3–4 PM); strong solar but chronic deficit overall
