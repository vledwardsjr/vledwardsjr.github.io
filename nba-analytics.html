Database & SQL Layer

Normalized PostgreSQL database covering 6 NBA seasons (2019-20 through 2024-25). 18 SQL views form a layered dependency chain — all scoring and award logic lives in SQL, not BI-layer DAX.

Composite Scoring System

Percentile-based composite scores at both player and team levels:

  • Player: Offensive Impact, Defensive Impact, Scoring Efficiency, Playmaking, Player Value Score
  • Team: Team Offense Score, Team Defense Score, Team Success Score

Award Framework

MVP, DPOY, and OPOY (real awards, kept) plus 5 fictional awards: Best Player, Best Scorer, Best Playmaker, and 4 fictional team awards. Several other categories were built, tested, and deliberately cut.

Deliverables

  • 7 Power BI dashboards: Player Season, Team Season, Season Awards, Player Comparison, Team Comparison, Player Progression, Team Progression
  • R Markdown methodology report (knitted to PDF)
  • Stakeholder PowerPoint deck
  • Full GitHub repository

Data sourced from Basketball Reference. Per their terms of service, raw data is excluded from the repository — only the database schema and SQL logic are published.

How the Scoring Works

Within-Season Percentiles

Each metric is converted to a percentile rank within its own season. A player's 2021-22 score is relative to other 2021-22 players, not the full historical pool. This accounts for pace and style drift across the 6-season span — raw stats from 2019-20 aren't directly comparable to 2024-25.

Weighted Addition, Not Multiplication

The first version multiplied component percentiles to compute the Player Value Score. This collapsed nearly every player's score toward zero: a 60th-percentile scorer × a 40th-percentile playmaker × a 50th-percentile defender already drops below 0.12. Rebuilt with weighted addition, anchored by VORP and WS/48.

Minutes & Games Floors

Rate stats per 100 possessions inflate for low-minute players. A 1,000-minute floor for standard seasons (600 for shortened seasons like 2019-20) filters out noise without excluding legitimate part-season players.

65-Game Eligibility

Individual awards require 65 games played, mirroring the NBA's own 2023-24 eligibility policy. This ensures award winners reflect a full season of performance, not a hot stretch.

Validation

2019-20 Best Defensive Team

The system ranked Bucks → Raptors → Lakers, matching the consensus defensive hierarchy for that season. Confirmed against live results.

OKC 2024-25 Defensive Season

Team Defense Score of 91.90, Net Rating +12.8, record 68–14. The system correctly identifies what was widely recognized as a historically dominant defensive season.

What Got Cut, and Why

× Most Improved Player

Year-over-year delta-percentile calculations compared players across season boundaries, but seasonal recalibration means the baseline shifts every year. The deltas weren't reliable enough to select a single winner.

× Sixth Man of the Year

Box-score data doesn't distinguish between a true bench scorer and a rotation player who starts a few games. Without play-by-play data to confirm bench time, the award couldn't be defensible.

× ROY & All-Rookie Teams

Rookies by definition have limited minutes. Without a minutes floor, Per-100-possession stats inflated for low-minute players, producing winners whose sample sizes didn't represent a full rookie campaign. Adding a floor would have excluded too many legitimate rookie seasons.

× Coach of the Year

Same delta structure as MIP — comparing coaching outcomes across season-recalibrated baselines — same reliability problem.

× All-Defense & All-Offensive Teams

Mathematically, these were just DPOY and OPOY rankings with a wider cutoff. They added categories without adding information.

× Early “Best Defensive Player”

A standalone defensive metric that predated the full system. Rate-stat inflation at low minutes made it unreliable — later superseded by DPOY with proper minutes floors.
